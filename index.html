About Me
.

I'm a British Web and Graphic Designer, all self-taught. Originally coming from music as a producer and DJ, I wanted to craft an identity for myself and the music I'm involved with, through colour, shapes, typography and ideas. I felt the urge to do something a little more than just making tunes. After some research and a bit of tinkering with HTML, CSS, a little bit in JavaScript and as well some programs such as Illustrator and Photoshop, I started to fall in love with design, alongside the music, and now I want to put my skills to the test and offer some solutions for others.

Arrgh

Website & branding for architect & artist Arrgh

Earlier this year I was approached by architect and visual curator Arrgh, to create a simple portfolio site to showcase the client's work. Focusing primarily on how to best present images, this project has been a great exercise in managing space and layout, spreading the content across multiple pages, rather than a busy one page layout.

Robert & Roberts

Website & Branding for Multi-Media company.

Robert & Roberts is a multimedia company based in East London, specialising in video and sound production for several high profile streetwear brands. I was approached with this project and handed an almost complete blank canvas. Examining the work they had done, in conjunction with the audience they wish to attract, I started to mockup the structure based around a select pallete of images and colours. Built using the Bootstrap framework, this one page site was a great exercise in developing a functioning one page site.

Sheffield F.C. Branding Concept

A fictional rebranding of the oldest Football Club.

With football being in its new era of rebranding, from the Premier League completely revamping their look, to Juventus adopting an incredibly modern looking new logo for the next season, I decided to give myself a task of attempting to rebrand the oldest running football club in the world.

Focusing on a design which incorporates all the key elements of the previous Sheffield F.C. crests, as well as adding some other elements reminiscent of early football and the city of Sheffield, I created a design entirely based on symbolism. Breaking down each of the componenets of the design we can cover which each means one by one.

From left to right, we can cover which symbol represents what. The football vector, coupled with the date of the club's founding, is a repesentation of the club's rich history and formation. The crossed arrows influenced from the Sheffield Coat of Arms. The half and half shield represents the two sides of football, a game of two sides of 11 men. And then the SFC emblem being a representation of the old common style of crest, still used by clubs such as Rangers, Queens Park Rangers and several clubs in Brazil such as Flamengo, Vitoria and Internacional.
